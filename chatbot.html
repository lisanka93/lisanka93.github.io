CovidVaccChatbot

Thank you for showing interest in chatting with CovidVaccBot!

You are being invited to take part in a research project. Before you decide it is important for you to understand why the research us being done and what participation will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully. Email us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Take time to decide whether or not you wish to take part.

Motivation and Purpose

As part of my PhD project on “Development of Chatbot Technology for Acquiring and Analysing Arguments” at University College London, we want to develop a chatbot as part of a toolkit for acquiring and analysing views of people on a variety of topics. Consider for example, in behaviour change, different people have different reasons for not eating a healthy diet or for not undertaking exercise. The aim of the chatbot would be to discuss these issues with different people in order to acquire data on the range of reasons. The dialogues acquired by the chatbot will be useful data for designing interventions in healthcare. In a sense, the chatbot will be an automated researcher for carrying out surveys with users.

Terms and Conditions

What will happen if you take part

Wou will engage in a dialogue with a chatbot about your opinion regarding COVID-19 vaccination. During the course of the dialogue the chatbot will present you with arguments for getting a COVID-19 vaccine, should one be developed and released to the public, to which you can reply with arguments that support your point of view.

Why have I been chosen?

Recruitment happens via Prolific. If you are able to sign up for this study it means you satisfy the inclusion criteria. You must be at least eighteen (18) years old to take part in this research study. You are responsible for providing your real age on prolific.

Do I have to take part?

Taking part in the study is entirely voluntary and you can discontinue at any time without giving a reason. Just stop replying to the chatbot and contact Lisa Chalaguine at lisa.chalaguine.16@ucl.ac.uk telling her you are withdrawing from the experiment, and she will delete your discussions from the database. You can contact us through prolific. It should be noted that withdrawal requests will not be accepted after the 1st of September 2020. If payment has not been made by the time the participant withdrew from the study, the participant will not be paid for his or her participation.

Information you submit

Neither in the MS Form, nor during the chat with the chatbot will you be asked for any personal information, so please refrain from doing so. We also guarantee that we will not use any content from your public facebook profile. No third parties have access to the facebook application and its chats and at the end of the experiment, the application including its chats will be deleted. By taking part in the study you promise that any information you submit either in the MS Form or to the chatbot is your honest, personal opinion. You grant us the permission to read, analyse and potentially publish any information you provide. You further agree that you shall not submit content in the MS Form or during the chat with the chatbot that is

  • obscene, vulgar, or pornographic
  • amounts to spam
  • is otherwise offensive or inappropriate by generally accepted standards

Violation will lead to the participant being blocked from accessing the chatbot.

Use of personal information

No personal data of you is collected during the study. The first legal basis for processing your data will be in the public interest. We shall securely store any information you supplyand may produce reports on the acquired data. These reports will be based on anonymous data, which means that individuals will not be identified in the reports. The information is stored locally on our server without the participants’ facebook names. Given that the subject of the study is COVID-19 related, and the data we collect may include medical information, the second legal basis is scientific and historical research or statistical purpose. Analysing people’s arguments and the concerns they have about the possible vaccine will help scientists and politicians to address those concerns.

Please refer to here for more information how researchers like us at UCL use your data.

Payment

You will be paid in pounds sterling via Prolific. The rate will be stated in the study details on Prolific.

Advantages of Participation

Expected benefits for participants are long term. If information acquisition could be automated, it would allow policy makers to act faster on the concerned topic. Instead of wasting time and resources identifying the reasons for the underlying problems, those resources could be spend on developing appropriate responsive measures.

Possible Risks of Participation

There may be a certain emotional risk related to reading and answering the chatbots arguments. Participants might get defensive or irritated when having to argue their point of view on vaccinations. However, the participant may choose not to respond and end the chat at any time and withdraw from the study (see next paragraph). Be aware that the cut-off date for withdrawing finished chats from the dataset is the 1st of September 2020 as by then we will have made the corpus publicly available for further research.

Raising a Complaint

If something goes wrong or you wish to complain about any part of the research study please contact Lisa Chalaguine at lisa.chalaguine.16@ucl.ac.uk. If your complaint has not been handled to your satisfaction you can contact the Chair of the UCL Research Ethics Committee at ethics@ucl.ac.uk.

If you wish to get hold of a copy of any published reports/papers of the research, please contact Lisa Chalaguine at lisa.chalaguine.16@ucl.ac.uk and request the paper on the study of interest.
